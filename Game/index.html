Learnagochi Design Journal

Storyline

  • You found an injured duck in a pond and chose to take care of it.
  • The duck becomes your learning guide and coding companion.
  • Every chapter is part of the duck’s recovery and your growth in programming.

Motivation (Intrinsic & Extrinsic)

  • Intrinsic: players build emotional attachment through bond level, feedback, and duck reactions.
  • Extrinsic: every chapter clear and post-assessment gives coins/points.
  • Higher scores give higher rewards, encouraging mastery and replay.

Levels / Chapters

  • Total of 5 chapters: Pond Basics, Variable Values, Logic Practice, Festival Showcase, Final Mastery.
  • Each chapter has its own animated scene, story hook, and challenge set.
  • Chapter 5 is the final mastery run, then the ending story (Scene 13-16 + The End) appears.

Engagement Elements (Why Players Keep Playing)

  • Drag-and-drop coding loop with instant feedback and animated duck reactions.
  • Chapter unlock flow creates progression and curiosity.
  • Coins/points and dress-up customization create long-term goals.

Programming Elements

  • Core concepts: string, bool, and int.
  • Mixed literals include tricky pairs like "true" vs true.
  • Variable-style values and type sorting support beginner coding foundations.

Basis / Design Rationale

  • Based on mastery learning: repeat, get feedback, improve, then progress.
  • Designed for beginner learners/target players who need guided and playful practice.
  • Combines narrative care-taking + coding practice to make learning memorable.
